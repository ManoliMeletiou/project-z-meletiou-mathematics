# Project Z World-Class Execution Blueprint

## 1. North-star outcome

Project Z will be a calm, game-like, evidence-led IB mathematics learning platform in which every learner:

1. completes a mandatory adaptive diagnostic before entering the main game;
2. receives an explainable pathway through prerequisite and target skills;
3. learns concepts through representations, worked examples, progressive hints and misconception repair;
4. practises with verified, meaningfully varied mathematics rather than repeated number swaps;
5. applies mathematics through inquiry, communication and authentic contexts;
6. revisits learning through mastery checks and spaced retrieval;
7. experiences progress as worlds, stages, missions and earned growth without confusing game rewards with grades.

“Better than any other mathematics app” is a target to prove, not marketing copy to publish prematurely. Project Z earns that position only through curriculum completeness, mathematical correctness, teaching effectiveness, engagement, accessibility, security, reliability and real-user evidence.

## 2. Curriculum truth and supported pathways

Project Z’s product matrix contains fourteen pathways:

| Programme | Product pathways |
|---|---|
| MYP Year 1 | Standard, Extended |
| MYP Year 2 | Standard, Extended |
| MYP Year 3 | Standard, Extended |
| MYP Year 4 | Standard, Extended |
| MYP Year 5 | Standard, Extended |
| DP Mathematics | AA SL, AA HL, AI SL, AI HL |

The official MYP framework covers number, algebra, geometry and trigonometry, statistics and probability, and supports Standard and Extended challenge. MYP schools retain flexibility over detailed year-by-year content and sequence. Therefore:

- Project Z must map every MYP skill to the current authorized IB guide and a documented school/product sequence.
- It must never describe its Year 1–5 sequence as an IB-issued year-by-year syllabus.
- Where a Year 1–3 Extended product pathway represents accelerated or enriched challenge, that status must be explicit and independently reviewed rather than presented as a separate IB-issued course.
- Every placement needs an authorized-source mapper and a different qualified mathematics educator before release.

MYP learning and assessment must cover all four real criteria:

| Criterion | Capability Project Z must develop and evidence |
|---|---|
| A — Knowing and understanding | Select and apply appropriate mathematics in familiar and unfamiliar situations, with correct reasoning and results. |
| B — Investigating patterns | Explore cases, identify and describe patterns, form rules or conjectures, verify them and justify conclusions at the appropriate year level. |
| C — Communicating | Use correct terminology, notation and representations; move coherently between them; show complete, concise reasoning. |
| D — Applying mathematics in real-life contexts | Identify relevant mathematics, construct and use models, reach context-sensitive conclusions, justify accuracy and limitations, and reflect on reasonableness. |

Criteria are not content silos. Each canonical mathematical skill records which criterion performances it can support, while longer B/C/D performances use structured tasks and rubrics. Automated feedback must expose evidence and uncertainty; it must not silently turn uncertain AI judgement into a formal result.

DP is versioned by cohort and actual IB specification:

- current first-assessment-2021 AA SL/HL and AI SL/HL remain distinct from first-assessment-2029 courses;
- AA emphasizes rigorous analysis, algebraic fluency, mathematical argument, functions and calculus, with and without technology as applicable;
- AI emphasizes modelling, statistics, interpretation, real-world problem solving and purposeful technology use;
- Project Z uses the assessment objectives, inquiry stages, paper rules and internal-assessment criteria belonging to the learner’s registered cohort;
- MYP Criteria A–D must not be falsely copied onto a DP cohort. For the first-assessment-2029 specification, the official inquiry stages and the new A–D exploration criteria are represented as published; the current course keeps its own applicable assessment model.

## 3. The canonical skill atlas

The atlas is the spine of the product. Every approved skill requires:

- stable identifier, version and lifecycle state;
- programme, cohort specification, pathway, year/level, topic, subtopic and intended order;
- precise learner-facing objective and success criteria;
- prerequisite and successor edges, including cross-pathway bridges;
- conceptual, procedural, representational and application components;
- notation, vocabulary, calculator/technology expectations and common misconceptions;
- MYP criterion strands or applicable DP assessment dimensions;
- diagnostic probes and evidence thresholds;
- teaching explanations, examples, hints and representation choices;
- question families, contexts, difficulty bands and response modes;
- accessibility and localization metadata;
- authorized-source locator, provenance, mapper decision and independent educator approval.

Coverage is computed only over approved canonical skills. Duplicate, orphaned, inactive, legacy or unverified rows never count.

## 4. Mandatory first diagnostic — the game prologue

The first diagnostic is the learner’s required entry point into the main game. It is not an optional quiz and it is not a high-stakes grade.

### Experience

1. **Welcome and safety:** explain that the diagnostic finds the best starting point and cannot lower a school grade.
2. **Pathway registration:** confirm programme, year/course, Standard/Extended or AA/AI SL/HL, cohort specification, language and approved accommodations.
3. **Tool orientation:** teach the input methods, calculator boundary, diagrams and “I don’t know yet” control with two unscored examples.
4. **Adaptive prologue:** begin with high-information anchor items, then move through prerequisite and target branches according to evidence.
5. **Misconception checks:** use carefully designed distractors, short explanations or follow-up probes to distinguish slips, guesses and conceptual gaps.
6. **Confidence and effort:** optionally collect non-punitive confidence; record hints, response time and revisions as evidence quality, never as shame or a grade.
7. **Pause and resume:** preserve state securely; support extra time, screen readers, keyboard-only use, reduced motion and readable mathematical notation.
8. **Completion:** require sufficient evidence across the registered pathway’s entry domains. The main map unlocks only when the diagnostic is complete; accommodations change access, not the evidence standard.
9. **Reveal:** show strengths, priority foundations, the first recommended mission and why it was selected. Do not label a learner as “bad at maths.”
10. **Teacher evidence:** provide an explainable report, uncertainty/confidence bands, attempted domains and recommended starting nodes. Teachers may add evidence and adjust the learning plan, but the original diagnostic record remains immutable.

### Mathematical validity

- Item selection maximizes useful information while avoiding unnecessary length.
- A single answer cannot prove mastery.
- Correct guesses, repeated retries, answer reveals and heavy hints reduce evidence weight.
- Branches stop only after the configured confidence threshold or an explicit inconclusive result.
- No diagnostic item is active until curriculum placement, answer verification, misconception mapping, accessibility review and human mathematics review pass.
- Placement is calibrated against teacher-reviewed cases before production release.

### Game consequence

The diagnostic builds the learner’s first map. Secure skills appear as discovered territory; priority gaps become reachable missions; advanced areas remain visible as future worlds without implying failure. Diagnostic performance never removes permanent access to learning.

## 5. The learning game

Project Z should feel like a coherent adventure, not a dashboard covered in unrelated badges.

### Game structure

- **Universe:** the learner’s programme and course.
- **Worlds:** major mathematical domains or course topics.
- **Regions:** coherent prerequisite clusters.
- **Stages:** canonical skills in teachable order.
- **Missions:** learn, guided practice, independent practice, application/inquiry and spaced review.
- **Checkpoints:** short mixed evidence checks after a cluster.
- **Boss challenges:** multi-skill, unfamiliar or authentic tasks that demand transfer; they are supportive mastery events, not artificial difficulty spikes.
- **Side quests:** enrichment, history, puzzles, modelling and interdisciplinary applications; clearly separate from required coverage.
- **Companion:** a learning guide that evolves through sustained, healthy learning behaviours and mastery evidence, never through spending or formal grades.

### Reward rules

- XP rewards meaningful actions: independent reasoning, correction, explanation, spaced review and perseverance.
- Levels represent game progression, never MYP/DP grades.
- Streaks use compassionate recovery and must not punish illness, holidays, disability or school schedules.
- Achievements recognize varied strengths, including reasoning, communication, application, reflection and helping oneself through corrections.
- Cosmetics, companion evolution and unlockable themes cannot confer assessment advantage.
- No loot boxes, paid randomness, manipulative scarcity, public shaming or pay-to-win design.
- Teacher-assigned work and formal reports clearly distinguish learning evidence from game rewards.

### Calm interaction contract

Each primary page has one dominant next action, at most two visible secondary actions and advanced tools behind progressive disclosure. The student should normally see: where they are, why this mission matters, and what to do next. Rich capability is available at the right depth, not placed in the learner’s face at once.

## 6. Practice depth and mathematical verification

Every active skill must support at least 2,000 mathematically distinct, reproducible, verified instances. This is a floor, not a claim based on superficial parameter substitution.

Each suitable skill needs multiple families across:

- conceptual meaning and procedural fluency;
- multiple representations and translation between them;
- forward, reverse, missing-information and error-analysis forms;
- familiar, unfamiliar and authentic contexts;
- non-calculator and technology-required modes where applicable;
- MYP A/B/C/D or applicable DP inquiry/assessment demands;
- difficulty, cognitive demand and reading-load progression;
- misconceptions that produce plausible but provably incorrect distractors;
- explanation, justification and reflection where the construct requires them.

Release requires parameter validation, independent answer computation, exact/symbolic/numerical equivalence, domain/unit/precision/notation checks, duplicate detection, distractor validation, ambiguity and accessibility checks, human mathematics approval, versioned evidence and automatic quarantine on regression.

## 7. The teaching AI

The tutor is a learning coach, not an answer machine. For every interaction it should:

1. identify the goal and inspect the learner’s attempt;
2. infer the likely misconception or missing prerequisite with stated confidence;
3. select the smallest useful representation or sub-step;
4. provide the least revealing helpful hint;
5. ask the learner to do something mathematical;
6. check reasoning, not only the final answer;
7. repair the misconception explicitly when evidence supports it;
8. summarize the transferable idea;
9. give a short independent check;
10. record structured, privacy-safe learning evidence.

The tutor must be curriculum- and skill-grounded, age-appropriate, prompt-injection resistant, rate/cost limited and unable to publish unverified assessment material. It must support mathematical notation first and use diagrams, graphs, manipulatives, audio or vision only when they improve understanding and remain accessible. Learning-gain and teacher-calibration evaluations are required before superiority claims.

## 8. Complete role loops

### Student

Account → required diagnostic → personalized map → teach → guided attempt → independent practice → feedback → correction → mastery → spaced review → checkpoint/boss → next mission.

### Teacher

Verified role → class/roster → diagnostic and mastery evidence → recommendation → controlled generation → deterministic audit → teacher review → publish → monitor → feedback/memorandum → correction review → report and pathway adjustment.

### Parent

Secure child link → parent-safe overview → understandable progress → upcoming learning and practical support actions. Never raw tutor chats, private notes, another learner’s data or game metrics presented as grades.

### Operator

Role approvals → curriculum/reviewer governance → content quarantine/release → security and incident monitoring → support, privacy and audit operations. Operators cannot approve their own curriculum evidence or bypass two-person mathematical review.

## 9. Technical and safety foundation

- Next.js/TypeScript frontend, Supabase/Postgres/Auth/RLS and the existing verified engine architecture remain the baseline unless an evidence-backed change is approved.
- Database roles, RLS, grants and server checks are authoritative; navigation is not security.
- Anonymous and cross-role access is denied by default.
- AI and generated content are untrusted until verified.
- Every production schema change is a migration with verification and rollback.
- CI blocks failures in tests, type checking, build, security contracts and production smoke checks.
- Child privacy, retention, export, deletion, consent, accessibility and incident response are launch requirements.

## 10. Risk-first delivery sequence

1. **Transfer and truth:** complete the authoritative handover, project instructions, first prompt, boundaries and current evidence.
2. **Authorized curriculum:** connect current legally accessed guides; version cohorts; verify reviewers; approve every placement in prerequisite order.
3. **Diagnostic vertical slice:** release one complete, reviewed entry domain from diagnostic through teaching, practice, mastery and game unlock; validate it with real learners before multiplying it.
4. **Generator scale:** build and independently verify multiple families and at least 2,000 instances for every approved skill.
5. **Assessment depth:** implement MYP A–D task types and applicable DP inquiry/paper/exploration demands without overstating automated judgement.
6. **Adaptive intelligence:** calibrate diagnostic selection, mastery, recommendations and spaced review against reviewed learning histories.
7. **Tutor quality:** evaluate misconception repair, hint quality, safety and measurable learning gain.
8. **Game system:** integrate worlds, stages, missions, checkpoints, boss challenges, companion and healthy rewards with the verified learning graph.
9. **Role completeness:** pass student, teacher, parent and operator loops with real RLS/browser evidence.
10. **Quality and operations:** WCAG 2.2 AA, device/performance budgets, monitoring, backups/restores, privacy/compliance and incident drills.
11. **Pilot and launch:** controlled representative pilot, zero release blockers, signed acceptance, tagged deployment and reproducible rollback.

Scaling thousands of questions before validating one end-to-end learning slice would multiply hidden defects. The vertical slice establishes the release pattern; the atlas then scales through automated tooling and independent review without lowering the gate.

## 11. Objective 100% decision

Project Z is 100% launch-complete only when every row in `PROJECT_Z_100_PERCENT_ACCEPTANCE_MATRIX.md` is green, every advertised pathway has approved coverage and practice depth, the diagnostic and complete role loops pass, mathematical and AI quality are independently evidenced, operations are tested, and a controlled pilot is signed. A large row count, attractive UI, successful build or live deployment cannot substitute for these gates.
